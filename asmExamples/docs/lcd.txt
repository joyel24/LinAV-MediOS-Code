The dsc25 has an integrated on screen display system (OSD). This document shows
how to make the most of it...

Overview
--------
    The dsc25 has the following segments:
    * background color
    * 2 video planes. These carry YCbCr data - 32 bit per pixel.
    * 2 bitmap planes. These carry CLUT indexes (16 bit per pixel).
    * 2 cursors.

The dsc25 can blend these segments together, or can say 'it's color 0 so I'll
show whats behind it'. The order is not changable. They display in the order
given above.
All SDRAM offsets are given relative to 0x3000000, and shifted right 5 places.
That means your video buffers must be aligned to a 32 byte boundary.

See breakout game... The background image is on video plane 1. The bricks and
text is on bitmap 1, the ball is on bitmap 2, and the paddle is a cursor.
This means the ball and paddle are just moved by setting the position!
